Temperature extremes are rare. "City X will hit 95°F today" is usually false. A NO position wins if the temperature stays below a stated threshold, which our models can predict with high confidence when every ensemble member across all four forecast models clusters far from the threshold. We never trade YES because the upside is smaller and the prediction is harder.
We keep it simple: one bankroll, not a tangle of slots or compounding ladders. Decide the total you're comfortable risking (your bankroll), then stake a small fixed fraction of it on each signal - many traders use 1-5%, scaled by the signal's confidence grade. Hold no more than two positions in any one weather region, and let the pre-set stop-loss cap the downside on each trade. As your bankroll grows, the same percentage naturally puts a little more to work; if it shrinks, you automatically bet less. The sizing calculator does the math for you.
